I am a Master of Computational Data Science student in the School of Computer Science at Carnegie Mellon University.

Before joining CMU, I conducted research in the Machine Learning Group in National Taiwan Univeristy, led by Prof. Chih-Jen Lin. My research was on the second-order optimization methods for deep neural networks, and the development of the solver for classifiers based on the neural nets. I designed and implemented the optimization algorithm that largely reduces the memory usage by exploiting the data structure of the model, so that the method could be applied to large-scale data.

During my work at SLAC National Accelerator Laboratory, I analyzed the astrophysical data coming from the European Space Agency, and built a model to categorize the behavior of the data. I also developed a numerical code for solving the non-linear system in astrophysics, and participated in improving the compatibility of the open-source code, CAMB, with non-parametrized data.
